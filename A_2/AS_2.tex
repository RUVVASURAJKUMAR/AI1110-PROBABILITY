\textbf{MISCELLINIOUS EXERCISE 16 : Q9}\\

\textbf{Question :} If 4-digit numbers greater than 5,000 are randomly formed from the digits 0,1,3,5 and 7, what is the probability of forming a number divisible by 5 when,


\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item the digits are repeated?
\item the repetition of digits is not allowed?
\end{enumerate}


\solution \\
Let $i$ represents digits of the four digit numbers where $i$ \in \cbrak{1,2,3,4}\\
$X_{i}$=represets occurence of 0,1,3,5 and 7 in the $i$th digit where $i$ \in \cbrak{1,2,3,4} \\


\begin{table}[ht!]
    \centering
    \input{tables/tabel_3.tex}
    \caption{}
\end{table}

To form a number divisible by 5, $X_{4}$ must be either 0 or 5. There are 2 favorable outcomes for $X_{4} out of the 5 possible values.

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item

	For $i\neq j $ where $i,j $ \in \cbrak{1,2,3,4}	
	
\begin{align}
	\pr{X_{i}=X_{j}}
\end{align}

\text Conditons:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Greater than 5000 $\Rightarrow X_{4} \in \{5,7\}$
    \item Divisible by 5 $\Rightarrow X_{1} \in \{0,5\}$
    \item Repetition allowed $\Rightarrow X_{2}$ and $X_{3} \in \{0,1,3,5,7\}$
\end{enumerate}


Total number of 4-digit numbers greater than 5000 &= 2 × 5 × 5 × 5 −1

Total number of 4-digit numbers greater than 5000 that are divisible by 5 &= 2 \times 5 \times 5\times 2 -1

\begin{align}
	\Rightarrow \pr{X_{i}=X_{j}} & = \frac{\brak{2 \times 5 \times 5 \times 2 - 1}}{\brak{2 \times 5 \times 5 \times 5 - 1}}\\ & = \frac{99}{249}.
\end{align}



\item
	For $ i\neq j $ \forall $ i,j $ \in \cbrak{1,2,3,4}
	
\begin{align}
	\pr{X_{i} \neq X_{j}}
\end{align}

\text There are three possible cases :

\begin{enumerate}
\item $X_{4}$ = 5\\
$X_{1}$ = 0\\
\item $X_{4}$ = 7\\
$X_{1}$ = 0\\
\item $X_{4}$ = 7\\
$X_{1}$ = 5\\
\item repetition not allowed $\Rightarrow$ $X_{2}$ and $X_{3}$ = two digits from the three left\\
\end{enumerate}

\begin{align}
	\Rightarrow \pr{X_{i} \neq X_{j}} & = \frac{\brak{6 + 6 + 6}}{\brak{2 \times 4 \times 3 \times 2}}\\ & = \frac{18}{48}\\ & = \frac{3}{8}.
\end{align}
}

\end{enumerate}
